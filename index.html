I-Non Chiu

Postdoc fellow in Academia Sinica Institute of Astronomy and Astrophysics (ASIAA)

Hello, I am an astrophysicist and cosmologist from Taiwan. My Chinese name is 邱奕儂. Welcome to my personal webpage.

I have been a postdoc working with Prof. Keiichi Umetsu in Academia Sinica Institute of Astronomy and Astrophysics (ASIAA) since the summer of 2016. Before then, I had studied my PhD working closely with my supervisor Prof. Dr. Joseph Mohr in Ludwig Maximilians University (LMU) in Munich for 4 years since 2012. Before moving to Munich, I started my scientific career and studied my Master Degree in National Taiwan University (NTU) and ASIAA working with Dr. Sandor Molnar and Prof. Pisin Chen.

My research interest lies in the field of cosmology and astrophysics, especially in the context of galaxy clusters using observations in multi-wavelength. My expertise spans a wide range of diversity on both observational and theoretical sides, including X-ray data analysis, optical and NIR photometry, gravitational lensing, photometry redshift estimation, CCD image reduction, Sunyaev–Zel'dovich effect (SZE) analysis, statistical modelling and cosmological implication.
